1.

we immediately commenced negotiations with all our landlords, resulting in deferral of a significant proportion of rents through the period of closure. Given our strong track record as a tenant at all our sites in many cases we have also achieved significant rent-free periods that we will benefit from during the remainder of 2020 and in many cases through into 2021. We are grateful to those landlords who showed understanding and help in this regard.

2.

all staff at all levels accepted a significant reduction in hours/ pay throughout the period of closure.

3.

furloughed all staff except for a small number of people to support the executive team.

In the months prior to reopening we developed a robust plan to ensure that we could re-open in a safe, COVID-19 secure way, ensuring the safety of both our people and our customers. Our large sites meant that we can readily adhere to strict social distancing measures, we have amended our operating protocols to reduce the amount of people in our sites at any one time, and restricted opening hours to ensure that our sites can be thoroughly cleaned before the next day’s trade.

In addition, we have invested significant capital to install ozone machines in each of our sites. This ensures that all customer equipment can be sanitised after every customer visit.


Post opening

Having been given the go ahead to open from the 4th July our priority remained the safety of our customers. To that end we managed opening carefully to ensure that we had senior resource available to manage re-opening effectively. The feedback from our customers has been excellent and reinforced the steps we have taken. Other than our Scottish tracks we were fully operational by the end of August.

Trading has been growing encouragingly since re-opening and is ahead of Budget, and despite ongoing restrictions to manage a second wave the resilience of the business has been good. Our scale, strong culture and focus on every detail gives me confidence that we will continue to surpass our customers’ expectations.

We are of course subject to potential further future lockdowns and are managing our cost contracts and our cash carefully.

This has been a very difficult period for everyone, and I would like to thank all our employees for their dedication and loyalty to the business throughout this turbulent year. Their response both during lockdown and since reopening has been excellent.

Strategic Report for the Year Ended 30 June 2020

The directors present their strategic report for the year ended 30 June 2020.

Principal activity

The principal activity of the group is managing and operating indoor go karting tracks.

The principal activity of the company is that of a holding company.

Fair review of the business

The results for the year which are set out in the profit and loss account show turnover of £27,240,615 (2019 - £32,702,529) and an operating loss of £6,572,054 (2019 - £2,276,853). At 30 June 2020 the group had total assets less current liabilities of £41,100,710 (2019 - £44,508,637).

As noted in the Chairman’s statement we had an excellent start to the year with strong like-for-like growth and a successful new opening in the first 8 months of the year. However, the spread of Covid-19 which resulted in a mandatory Government lock down of much of the leisure and tourism sector on 20 March 2020, meant that we were unable to trade until the end of our financial year, over 3 months. This clearly impacted the final result for the year.

We had anticipated a lock down, and the inevitable loss of revenue, and took proactive steps. Prior to lock down all our employees agreed to a 20% reduction in pay or hours. We entered negotiations with all landlords and in most cases were able to agree deferred rent terms, as well as rent free holidays. We engaged with HMRC, and over the following months agreed deferred payment terms.

The Government offered significant assistance, and the speed in which the initiatives were established, and then delivered has been of tremendous benefit. The ability to access the furlough scheme has been fundamental to this. Given the complete shutdown of the business we were able to furlough over 95% of our workforce. Through proactive communication we ensured that we kept the team engaged, and loyal to our business, which has really benefitted us as we reopened. In addition to the furlough scheme we benefited from the rates holiday given to the leisure sector, providing immediate and sustained support.

We also immediately put a stop to all major capital projects, and thanks to our strong supplier relationships we have been able to do this without any significant incremental cost to the business.

In addition to these actions we have the benefit of a strong financial position. We entered the crisis with healthy cash reserves, having been able to access existing facilities where we had a good level of headroom. However, the nature of the pandemic means that at no point could we be complacent. With the support of our debt and equity providers post year end we have been able to increase our facilities. In addition we have simplified our covenants, with just one cash based covenant test until December 2021. We also extended the term of our facilities by a further 2 years until November 2025, giving us significant stability for the years ahead.

Collectively the measures we have taken have given the business significant stability, and as a board we were confident that we could weather a prolonged lockdown.

Fortunately we were able to reopen on the 4th July. However, we took a considered approach, and opened our tracks in phases.

During the period of lockdown we developed a detailed opening strategy, with the safety of our staff and our customers at the heart of everything we did. We engaged with local EHO officers to seek guidance on our plans, we reduced capacity at our sites to ensure social distancing both on and off track, and invested in specialist cleaning equipment across our estate.

We are pleased with the progress made, but we recognise that we face a volatile period ahead, with no one approach fitting every potential outcome. Despite this, we remain confident in our ability to manage what is an evolving situation on an almost daily basis.

In the short term we do expect further disruption. However, we are grateful to have a loyal customer base that has helped underpin our success. In addition, our ability to offer a safe environment, when other operators in the leisure sector are not able to operate, provides a significant sales opportunity.
